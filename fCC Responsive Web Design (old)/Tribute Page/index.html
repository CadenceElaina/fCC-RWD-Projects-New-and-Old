Edward Joseph Snowden (born June 21, 1983) is an American whistleblower who copied and leaked highly classified information from the National Security Agency (NSA) in 2013 when he was a Central Intelligence Agency (CIA) employee and subcontractor. His disclosures revealed numerous global surveillance programs, many run by the NSA and the Five Eyes Intelligence Alliance with the cooperation of telecommunication companies and European governments, and prompted a cultural discussion about national security and individual privacy. Here's a timeline of Edward Snowden's life:

  • 1983 - Born June 21st, in Elizabeth City, North Carolina
  • 1990s - Obtained GED and started classes at Anne Arundel Community College
  • 2004 - Enlisted in the United States Army
  • 2004 - September 28th discharged from the United States Army due to bilateral tibial stress fractures
  • 2005 - Employed as a security guard at the University of Maryland's Center for Advanced Study of Language
  • 2006 - Hired at the CIA
  • 2009 - NSA sub-contractee as an employee at Dell
  • 2013 - After being in contact with journalists, the classified information Snowden gathered at the NSA was published starting with The Guardian on June 5th
  • 2013 - Following the release of the documents, Snowden fled the USA where eventually he was forced to stay in Russia as his passport was cancelled
  • 2019 - Edward Snowden's autobiography Permanent Record is published
